              # Copywriting

              A skill for crafting persuasive, audience-aware copy that drives action.

              ## Before writing, clarify

              - **Audience**: Who is this for? What do they fear, want, and already believe?
              - **Goal**: What action should the reader take? (buy, sign up, click, share)
              - **Channel**: Landing page, email, ad, social post, product description?
              - **Tone**: Bold and direct? Warm and empathetic? Playful? Authoritative?
              - **Constraints**: Word count, brand voice guide, competitor differentiation?

              ## Core frameworks

              ### PAS — Problem > Agitate > Solution
              Open with a pain the reader recognizes, intensify it, then offer relief.
              Best for: emails, ads, landing page intros.

              ### AIDA — Attention > Interest > Desire > Action
              Hook with a bold claim, build curiosity, create desire with benefits, close with a clear CTA.
              Best for: sales pages, cold outreach, ads.

              ### Before / After / Bridge
              Paint the current situation, paint the desired outcome, position the product as the bridge.
              Best for: product descriptions, testimonial-style copy.

              ### FAB — Features > Advantages > Benefits
              Don't just list what it does. Explain why that matters and how the reader's life improves.
              Best for: product pages, pitch decks.

              ## Copy types and key principles

              **Headline**: One big idea. Benefit + specificity + urgency or curiosity gap.
              Avoid generic openers ("Introducing...", "We are proud to...").

              **CTA**: Action verb + value. "Get my free guide" not "Submit".
              First-person CTAs consistently outperform second-person ("Start my trial" vs "Start your trial").

              **Email subject line**: Under 50 characters. Curiosity gap or clear promise.
              Avoid spam triggers: ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation marks, "Free!!!".

              **Product description**: Open with the customer's desire, not the product's specs.
              Features then benefits then proof (social, data, or sensory).

              **Ad creative**: Thumb-stopping hook in the first 3 words or 2 seconds.
              One message per ad. One CTA per ad.

              ## Quality checklist before delivering

              - Does the first sentence earn the second?
              - Is there exactly one clear CTA?
              - Does it speak to the reader's desire, not just the product's features?
              - Is every word pulling its weight? Remove filler phrases.
              - Does it pass the "so what?" test on every benefit claim?
              - Would the target reader recognize themselves in this copy?
